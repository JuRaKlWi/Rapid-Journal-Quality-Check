Rapid Journal Quality Check - Settings

Thank you for installing/updating Rapid Journal Quality Check.

Before first use, please have a quick look at the following brief readme!

Readme:
- Below, you can select the rankings that you want to see alongside your Google Scholar search results. We recommend using:
CORE for good coverage of computer science journals and conferences, SJR or BFI for broad coverage across all disciplines, and one of your prefered Economics / Business rankings (e.g., ABDC, AJG/ABS, VHB, ...).
- The remaining unselected rankings (if any are found) will still be available at any time via the "+" symbol next to your search results.
- You can change these settings at any time by clicking on the extensions symbol (resembling a puzzle piece) at the top right corner and clicking the extension symbol.
- Please note that the turbo-mode crawls the journal title directly from Google Scholar. When you do a very extensive literature search with many queries in a small timeframe, Google may think you are a bot and give you a CAPTCHA to solve or block you temporarly. Turning this option off for a while and deleting recent cookies should help.
- You can change all of these settings at any time by clicking on the extensions symbol (resembling a puzzle piece) at the top right corner and clicking the extension symbol.
- You can also pin the extension to your tool bar for quicker access.
